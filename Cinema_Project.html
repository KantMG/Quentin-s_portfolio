The seventh art

OBJECTIVE

This project enlights the evolution over the years of movie and series making. The adaptation of the way of production as well as our way of consumption are analyzed.

Information on the dataframe

The IMDb Non-Commercial Datasets have been used to perform this study, the open source can be found below.

Code development

Creation of a user-friendly Dash interface for the IMDb dataframe analysis.

  • The Python library Pandas is used for data manipulation.
  • Data extraction and merging between the many data files are optimized.
  • The interface offers a wide range of plot management thanks to many Dash callback functions.
  • Available include data filtration, machine learning regressions, smoothing with the Savitzky-Golay filter, variable creation, dynamic subplot modification.

Work achievement

  • Historical Analysis of Production Trends: The study tracks the evolution of film and series production from the inception of cinema in the late 19th century to the present day. It identifies significant production trends, including the dominance of long movies in the early to mid-20th century and the exponential increase in series production since the 1950s.
  • Genre Diversity and Audience Engagement: The study categorizes productions into various genres and analyzes audience preferences over time. It reveals a significant diversity in genres, especially in series production, demonstrating changing viewer interests towards more immersive storytelling and character development.
  • Directorial Trends: The report investigates the age and diversity of directors' works, noting a gradual decrease in the average age of directors at their first production. It analyzes how the number of genres explored by directors has evolved, showing a decline in the average diversity per director over the years.
  • Comparative Analysis of Ratings: The study compares the ratings of movies and series, finding that series often achieve higher ratings than films, suggesting a shift in audience preferences. It also considers the role of viewer engagement metrics, such as the number of votes, in establishing trends in production quality and audience reception.
